1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit our website. They help us recognize your device and remember certain information about your preferences or past actions.

Similar technologies include web beacons, pixels, and local storage, which we may also use to enhance your experience on our platform.

üç™ Quick Fact

Cookies cannot harm your device or access your personal files. They simply store small pieces of information to make your browsing experience better.

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to provide you with the best possible learning experience. Specifically, we use them to:

  • Keep you logged in: Remember your login session so you don't have to sign in repeatedly
  • Save your progress: Track your course progress and resume where you left off
  • Personalize content: Show you relevant courses and recommendations based on your interests
  • Improve performance: Load pages faster and provide a smoother user experience
  • Analyze usage: Understand how our platform is used to make improvements
  • Ensure security: Protect against fraudulent activity and maintain platform security

3. Types of Cookies We Use

We categorize cookies based on their purpose and duration:

Session Cookies

Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser. These are essential for basic functionality.

Persistent Cookies

Remain on your device for a specified period or until you delete them. These remember your preferences across visits.

First-Party Cookies

Set directly by Nihongo Sekai to provide core functionality and improve your experience.

Third-Party Cookies

Set by external services we use, such as analytics providers or video hosting platforms.
